Low-tech for the uncertain

A research lab focused on climate resilient, rugged and low-power technologies for community-owned infrastructure & services. Our research demystifies ubiquitous technologies and investigates their energy consumption and environmental impact, in order to collectively re-imagine services and infrastructure through the lens of participatory climate resilience.

PEOPLE

Benedetta Piantella

Professor

Benedetta is a designer turned educator and humanitarian technologist. She was involved in international development projects for the past fifteen years, ever since her experience of surviving the Tsunami in Sri Lanka in 2004 and organizing relief efforts from the ground. She has been teaching for almost two decades in different disciplines and to different age groups, from STEM and Robotics courses to K-12 students to Ideation & Prototyping, HCI, Physical Computing and Global Engineering classes to undergraduate and graduate students.

COURSES

Solar Powered Media VIP

Spring 2021 / Fall 2021

Students in this VIP Team learn how to set-up a solar-powered server, receive hands-on experience with the hardware and software necessary to power a solar-powered website and investigate the environmental impact of online media content and Web design.

View syllabus

Practical Guide to Resilience

Fall 2020/Spring 2021

This is a “choose-your-own-adventure” course that merges citizen science, physical computing and design research and aims at reframing resource constraints and disruptions as re-thinking and design opportunities.

View syllabus

UNChained

Fall 2018

In this course, in partnership with the UNICEF Ventures Fund, we investigate how to assess the potential of new and emerging technologies – in particular blockchain, smart contracts and tokens – and the role they can play in addressing some of the most pressing challenges facing the most vulnerable population in the world: children.
